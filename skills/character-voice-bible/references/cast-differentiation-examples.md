# Cast Differentiation Examples

Use this file when speakers still overlap after basic cleanup.

## Example 1: Same Function, Different Reaction

Shared situation: both characters must refuse a risky request.

- Speaker A: refuses fast, narrows options, protects status distance
- Speaker B: circles around refusal, softens the blow, leaks worry

The informational outcome is the same, but the social behavior is different.

## Example 2: Same Emotion, Different Exposure

Shared situation: both characters are jealous.

- Speaker A: asks a practical question that is obviously not practical
- Speaker B: becomes overly helpful and passive-aggressive

Do not make both characters say the word for the feeling.

## Example 3: Same Hierarchy, Different Texture

Shared situation: both are subordinates speaking upward.

- Speaker A: formally compliant but quietly resistant
- Speaker B: respectful on the surface, eager for approval underneath

Status alone does not determine voice. Desire direction matters.

## Distinctness Test

If you remove names from a five-turn exchange, ask:

- who tries to control the pace
- who reveals more than intended
- who protects face
- who escalates
- who exits the exchange first

If the answers are interchangeable, rebuild the pair from reaction patterns and relationship stakes.
