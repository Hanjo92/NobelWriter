# Dialogue Exchange Patterns

Use this file when distinctness should be designed at the exchange level rather than the single-line level.

## Confrontation

Pattern:

1. accusation or pressure
2. denial, deflection, or counterattack
3. sharper proof or emotional hit
4. retreat, escalation, or partial admission

Keep the speakers asymmetrical. One should usually control pace while the other controls ambiguity.

## Flirtation Under Restraint

Pattern:

1. low-risk probe
2. dodge or dry return
3. second probe with slightly more exposure
4. partial opening that still preserves face

Do not let both speakers become equally candid at the same speed.

## Concealment

Pattern:

1. question
2. incomplete answer
3. push for specificity
4. substitute answer or pressure redirection

Hide the secret through structure, not through random vagueness.

## Persuasion

Pattern:

1. refusal
2. practical or emotional leverage
3. second refusal with weakened footing
4. compromise, threat, or reluctant shift

Differentiate speakers by what kind of leverage they trust.

## Apology

Pattern:

1. accusation or hurt
2. apology attempt
3. rejection or demand for real cost
4. more specific admission or changed behavior

A clean "I'm sorry" is usually not the real end of the exchange.
