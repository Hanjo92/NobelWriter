# Genre Voice Samples

Use this file when the same cast problem needs a different dialogue texture depending on genre.

## How To Use These Samples

Do not copy the lines directly unless the user asks for adaptation.

Use them to calibrate:

- how quickly emotion becomes explicit
- how much subtext the genre tolerates
- how visible hierarchy should be
- how compressed or expansive turns should feel
- how much exposition can ride inside dialogue before it sounds artificial

## Contemporary Romance

Shared situation: one character arrives late after a worrying near-miss.

### Flat version

```text
A: 왜 이제 왔어. 걱정했잖아.
B: 미안. 일이 있었어.
```

### Genre-tuned version

```text
A: 연락 한 통은 할 수 있었잖아.
B: 알아. 미안해.
A: 미안하단 말 말고.
B: ...다음엔 안 그럴게.
```

### Texture notes

- Keep emotional stakes close to the surface, but do not let confession become fully explicit too fast.
- Let care appear through complaint, hesitation, and partial demands.
- Favor short turns with pressure residue.

## Palace Or Historical Romance

Shared situation: a lower-status character warns a superior about danger.

### Flat version

```text
A: 지금 가면 위험해요.
B: 괜찮아. 가야 해.
```

### Genre-tuned version

```text
A: 마마, 지금 행차하시면 아니 되옵니다.
B: 물러서라. 이미 늦었다.
A: 늦었더라도 막아야 하옵니다.
```

### Texture notes

- Status and address must stay legible at every turn.
- Emotional urgency should pass through duty, restraint, and formal insistence.
- Avoid modern casual softness unless the relationship has already broken protocol.

## Modern Thriller

Shared situation: two allies realize one of them hid critical information.

### Flat version

```text
A: 왜 숨겼어.
B: 말할 시간이 없었어.
```

### Genre-tuned version

```text
A: 왜 뺐어. 내가 모르게.
B: 말하면 움직였을 테니까.
A: 그래서 혼자 갔냐.
B: 넌 말렸을 거고, 난 시간 없었어.
```

### Texture notes

- Turns should feel efficient and loaded.
- Strip ornamental emotion words; let accusation and justification carry the heat.
- Keep exposition fragmented and tactical.

## Fantasy Or Regression Action

Shared situation: a leader rejects a reckless plan before a major battle.

### Flat version

```text
A: 그건 너무 위험해.
B: 하지만 해야 해.
```

### Genre-tuned version

```text
A: 안 된다. 지금 전력으로 그 문을 열면 후열이 비어.
B: 이번이 아니면 기회 없습니다.
A: 기회보다 생존이 먼저다. 명령이다.
```

### Texture notes

- Make command structure and stakes clear quickly.
- Dialogue can carry tactical information, but each speaker still needs a distinct posture toward risk.
- Let authority sound structural, not merely louder.

## School Or Youth Drama

Shared situation: one friend confronts another about spreading a rumor.

### Flat version

```text
A: 너 그 얘기하고 다녔어?
B: 아니, 그냥 조금 말했어.
```

### Genre-tuned version

```text
A: 너 그 말, 진짜 하고 다녔냐.
B: 야, 크게 퍼질 줄은 몰랐지.
A: 몰랐다고 끝나?
B: 그럼 어쩌라고. 나만 들은 얘기도 아니었는데.
```

### Texture notes

- Keep turns more immediate and overlap-friendly.
- Social face, embarrassment, and peer pressure should move the line endings.
- Do not over-polish; some roughness is part of the age texture.

## Office Drama

Shared situation: a junior pushes back on a senior's bad decision in a meeting aftermath.

### Flat version

```text
A: 그 결정은 잘못됐어요.
B: 내가 알아서 할게요.
```

### Genre-tuned version

```text
A: 선배님, 아까 그 방향은 다시 보셔야 할 것 같습니다.
B: 지금 와서 뒤집자는 거야?
A: 뒤집자는 게 아니라, 그대로 가면 팀이 다 떠안습니다.
```

### Texture notes

- Respect markers should remain, but strategic resistance must still be audible.
- Conflict often rides inside hedging rather than blunt attack.
- The more corporate the setting, the more subtext hides inside procedural wording.

## Genre Drift Warnings

- Romance that explains feelings too cleanly too early loses tension.
- Thriller dialogue collapses when everyone starts explaining complete backstory in full sentences.
- Historical dialogue breaks when modern intimacy or slang appears without a clear breach.
- Fantasy action dialogue flattens when rank and tactical posture disappear.
- Youth drama sounds false when every teenager speaks in polished authorial symmetry.
- Office drama loses bite when all disagreement becomes openly confrontational.
