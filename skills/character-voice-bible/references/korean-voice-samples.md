# Korean Voice Samples

Use this file when abstract rules are not enough and the user needs concrete Korean dialogue contrast.

## Sample 1: Same Refusal, Different Social Handling

Shared situation: both characters must refuse a dangerous proposal from the same ally.

### Flat version

```text
A: 그건 위험해서 안 돼.
B: 맞아. 너무 위험해.
```

### Differentiated version

```text
A: 안 돼. 그걸 지금 건드리면 다 같이 끝장이야.
B: 위험한 건 맞는데요, 지금 그쪽으로 가면 수습이 안 됩니다. 다른 길부터 보죠.
```

### What changed

- A refuses fast and closes the option immediately.
- B agrees with the risk but redirects with status-aware softening.
- The distinction comes from pace and hierarchy handling, not from fancy vocabulary.

## Sample 2: Same Anger, Different Exposure

Shared situation: both characters feel betrayed by the same teammate.

### Flat version

```text
A: 너 진짜 실망이야.
B: 나도 실망했어.
```

### Differentiated version

```text
A: 너, 그 말 하고도 여기 서 있을 수 있냐.
B: 실망은 제가 했죠. 그런데도 지금은 따질 때가 아니잖아요.
```

### What changed

- A attacks the other person's face and presence directly.
- B contains anger inside formal control, then reframes the timing.
- Both are angry, but only one speaker lets the emotion lead the turn.

## Sample 3: Romance Shift Without Sudden Collapse

Shared situation: a guarded pair moves one step closer after a near-miss.

### Flat version

```text
A: 이제 솔직해져도 되지?
B: 응, 나도 좋아해.
```

### Differentiated version

```text
A: 오늘 같은 일 또 생기면... 나 진짜 못 버텨.
B: 그런 말, 갑자기 하면 곤란해요.
A: 곤란하면 듣지 마.
B: 듣기 싫다는 뜻은 아니에요.
```

### What changed

- The register does not drop all at once.
- Intimacy advances through hesitation, partial admission, and pressure leakage.
- The scene shows movement through what each speaker still cannot say cleanly.

## Sample 4: Hierarchy Drift Repair

Shared situation: a subordinate pushes back against a superior in a crisis.

### Drifted version

```text
부하: 너 지금 판단 잘못했어.
상사: 내가 알아서 해.
```

### Repaired version

```text
부하: 팀장님, 지금 결정은 다시 보셔야 합니다.
상사: 선 넘지 마.
부하: 선 넘는 거 압니다. 그래도 이건 말씀드려야 합니다.
```

### What changed

- The subordinate does not become intimate just because the scene is urgent.
- Pushback appears through insistence and risk-taking, not through casual address.
- The repair preserves tension while keeping hierarchy legible.

## Sample 5: Drift Audit And Minimal Repair

Shared situation: later chapters made two rivals sound identical.

### Before

```text
민준: 왜 말 안 했어.
서윤: 너도 말 안 했잖아.
민준: 그래서 더 화나.
서윤: 나도 화나.
```

### After

```text
민준: 왜 숨겼어. 내가 모를 거라고 봤냐.
서윤: 말 안 한 건 맞아. 근데 네가 들을 상태도 아니었어.
민준: 판단은 내가 해.
서윤: 늘 그렇게 말하지. 그래서 내가 입 다문 거고.
```

### What changed

- 민준 drives pace and control.
- 서윤 blocks, absorbs, then returns pressure through justification.
- The lines no longer mirror each other's sentence logic.

## Sample Usage Rule

Do not copy a sample line directly into the user's manuscript unless the user explicitly asks for adaptation.

Use the samples to show:

- how reaction direction separates speakers
- how register can shift without breaking plausibility
- how to repair drift with the smallest possible change
