# Micro Voice: Catchphrases And Fillers

Use this file when a speaker's small repeated wording needs to be controlled.

## Core Rule

Treat catchphrases as garnish, not identity.

Good micro markers:

- appear under the right pressure
- do not show up in every turn
- reinforce status, avoidance, or temperament
- stay short enough not to hijack the line

## Use Cases

- habitual fillers such as "그니까", "아니", "뭐", "그러니까"
- recurring softeners such as "같은데요", "일단", "좀"
- dry verbal tags such as "됐고", "그래서", "그건"

## Sample Contrast

```text
A: 아니, 그건 지금 얘기할 게 아니고.
B: 됐고. 본론만 말해.
```

## What To Check

- would removing the phrase break the voice or improve the line
- is the phrase tied to a specific emotional condition
- are two characters sharing the same filler too often

## Drift Warning

If a reader can identify the speaker only because of one repeated word, the voice architecture is weak.
