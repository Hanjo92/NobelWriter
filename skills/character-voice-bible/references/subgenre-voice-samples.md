# Subgenre Voice Samples

Use this file when broad genre labels are too loose and the dialogue needs Korean web-fiction subgenre texture.

Exclude BL and GL calibration from this file. Route those elsewhere if the user explicitly asks for them.

## How To Use These Samples

Use the same cast problem, then tune:

- how overtly desire or ambition appears
- how strongly rank and role shape line endings
- how much tactical information fits inside one turn
- how polished, rough, or heightened the emotional surface should be
- how much the subgenre permits melodrama, restraint, or game-like brevity

Do not copy these lines directly into a manuscript unless the user asks for adaptation.

## Modern Romance

Shared situation: one person arrives late after causing worry.

### Subgenre-tuned version

```text
A: 연락도 없이 지금 와?
B: 알아. 미안해.
A: 미안한 건 아는데, 나 또 기다리게 하진 마.
B: ...안 그럴게. 진짜.
```

### Texture notes

- Let care appear through hurt and complaint.
- Keep confession partial unless the scene is a true emotional release.
- Use short turns with lingering aftertaste.

## Romance Fantasy

Shared situation: a retainer tries to stop a high-status lead from entering danger.

### Subgenre-tuned version

```text
A: 아가씨, 지금 나가시면 안 됩니다.
B: 비켜. 확인해야 해.
A: 확인은 제가 하겠습니다. 아가씨는 뒤로 물러서세요.
```

### Texture notes

- Keep status visible, but let devotion sharpen the line.
- Emotional attachment often appears as control, protection, or formal insistence.
- Do not modernize the texture unless the setting already supports it.

## Regression Romance

Shared situation: one character hears a line that meant something very different in the previous timeline.

### Subgenre-tuned version

```text
A: 이번엔 그렇게 안 보내.
B: 무슨 뜻이죠.
A: 지난번처럼 놓치진 않겠다는 뜻이야.
B: ...전 그 지난번을 모르는데요.
```

### Texture notes

- Let one speaker carry hidden temporal weight the other cannot decode.
- Subtext often comes from asymmetry of memory, not just hierarchy or romance.
- Avoid explaining the timeline in full inside the exchange.

## Hunter Or Modern Fantasy Action

Shared situation: the team leader rejects a reckless move before entering a gate.

### Subgenre-tuned version

```text
A: 안 된다. 지금 들어가면 힐러 라인 빈다.
B: 보스 패턴 끊을 기회가 지금뿐입니다.
A: 기회보다 복귀가 먼저야. 대열 유지해.
```

### Texture notes

- Turns should be compact, tactical, and rank-aware.
- Authority sounds clean and operational, not merely angry.
- Insert system or combat information only where pressure justifies it.

## Academy Fantasy

Shared situation: one student confronts another after a forbidden act nearly causes disaster.

### Subgenre-tuned version

```text
A: 너, 그 술식 혼자 건드렸냐.
B: 확인만 하려던 거였어.
A: 확인하다가 교실째 날릴 뻔했잖아.
B: ...그래서 실패한 거, 지금 인정하잖아.
```

### Texture notes

- Keep youth immediacy, but let setting-specific jargon sharpen the exchange.
- Peer rivalry often mixes embarrassment, pride, and real danger.
- Do not let everyone sound like adult strategists.

## Murim

Shared situation: a junior opposes a senior's decision before a duel.

### Subgenre-tuned version

```text
A: 사형, 지금 결투를 받으시면 함정입니다.
B: 물러나라. 피할 일은 아니다.
A: 피하는 것이 아니라 고르는 것입니다.
```

### Texture notes

- Status and sect culture should shape address and restraint.
- Conviction tends to appear through compressed dignity rather than chatter.
- Keep martial stakes clean; do not bloat the line with exposition.

## Thriller Or Crime

Shared situation: one partner learns the other concealed evidence.

### Subgenre-tuned version

```text
A: 증거, 왜 뺐어.
B: 네가 보면 바로 움직일 테니까.
A: 그래서 혼자 덮으려고 했냐.
B: 덮으려던 게 아니라, 시간 벌려고 한 거야.
```

### Texture notes

- Favor clipped accusation and defensive reframing.
- Information should arrive in fragments under pressure.
- Avoid fully processed emotional vocabulary.

## Revenge Drama

Shared situation: one character refuses to forgive after the other asks for a pause.

### Subgenre-tuned version

```text
A: 이제 와서 그만하자고?
B: 여기서 더 가면 너도 무너져.
A: 무너진 건 진작이야. 네가 모른 척했을 뿐이지.
```

### Texture notes

- Let resentment carry memory and rhythm.
- Emotional lines can be sharper and more declarative than in soft romance.
- Keep the voice controlled enough to remain pointed, not merely loud.

## Office Drama

Shared situation: a junior challenges a senior's bad call after a meeting.

### Subgenre-tuned version

```text
A: 선배님, 방금 결정은 다시 보셔야 할 것 같습니다.
B: 지금 와서 방향 바꾸자는 거야?
A: 바꾸자는 게 아니라, 이대로 가면 실무가 전부 뒤집힙니다.
```

### Texture notes

- Conflict often hides inside procedure, risk framing, and responsibility language.
- Respect markers stay in place even when resistance hardens.
- Do not make corporate disagreement sound like schoolyard shouting.

## Makjang Or High-Melodrama Family Drama

Shared situation: one family member exposes a long-hidden truth at dinner.

### Subgenre-tuned version

```text
A: 그만 숨기세요. 오늘은 끝까지 들을 겁니다.
B: 지금 여기서 그 얘길 꺼내겠다는 거니.
A: 여기서 아니면 또 묻힐 테니까요.
```

### Texture notes

- Heighten pressure and confrontation, but keep turn ownership clear.
- Melodrama allows stronger declarations, yet hierarchy and family address still matter.
- Distinction should come from who corners, who evades, and who performs control.

## Subgenre Drift Warnings

- Romance fantasy flattens when devotion, class, and danger stop shaping the same line.
- Regression dialogue breaks when the timeline burden is explained too literally.
- Hunter dialogue sounds fake when everyone narrates mechanics like a manual.
- Academy fantasy fails when students lose age texture and all speak like veteran commanders.
- Murim dialogue collapses when sect status and restraint disappear.
- Revenge drama weakens when bitterness becomes generic shouting.
- Makjang loses edge when everyone escalates at the same pitch.
