# Two-Person Dialogue

Use this file when the scene is a tight one-on-one exchange.

## Core Rule

Every line should hit the other speaker directly.

Two-person dialogue weakens when:

- one line could be removed without changing pressure
- both speakers start matching rhythm too neatly
- the exchange explains context instead of fighting over it
- both characters become equally explicit at the same time

## Common Shapes

### Pressure And Dodge

```text
A: question or accusation
B: dodge
A: narrower pressure
B: partial answer or counter
```

### Probe And Retreat

```text
A: low-risk probe
B: dry answer
A: stronger probe
B: retreat, joke, or partial opening
```

### Refusal And Leverage

```text
A: refusal
B: emotional or practical leverage
A: second refusal
B: threat, plea, or compromise
```

## Sample

```text
지후: 너 어제 왜 안 왔어.
나림: 일이 있었어.
지후: 그 말로 끝낼 거면 먼저 전화했어야지.
나림: 전화하면 올 거잖아. 그래서 못 했어.
```

## What To Check

- who owns pace
- who exposes more than intended
- who forces the topic narrower
- who leaves the last emotional residue
