# Greenfield Architecture

Use this file when turning a premise, hook, or short synopsis into a scalable longform plan from scratch.

## Expansion Order

Expand outward in this order:

1. story engine
2. structure mode, if relevant
3. genre package selection
4. series brief
5. story bible
6. series expansion map
7. volume ladder
8. first volume or first major arc plan
9. chapter-range plan

Do not jump from premise directly to 100 chapter summaries.

## Structure Mode Rule

If the user wants `옴니버스`, rotating-focus, or similar architecture, lock that as structure mode before the volume ladder.

This changes how focal characters rotate and how arcs close, but it does not replace genre-package choice.

## Choose One Main Expansion Logic

Pick the main way the story grows:

- power progression: the hero climbs but the cost and ceiling must grow too
- revenge deepening: each answer exposes a larger chain of responsibility
- romance escalation: intimacy, mistrust, hierarchy, and social cost keep changing
- mystery layering: each reveal creates a larger question, not just a smaller clue
- political spread: factions, institutions, and alliances widen the field
- family widening: inheritance, loyalty, and generational debt expand the cast

## Volume Design Rule

Each volume or major arc should do one primary job:

- prove the engine works
- widen the arena
- fracture the core relationship
- expose the hidden rule
- reverse who holds power
- narrow the road to the ending

If every volume tries to do all jobs equally, the shape blurs.

## Genre Package Before Volume Design

Choose the dominant genre package before designing volume order. The same premise can produce different ladders:

- romance fantasy: volume one may deepen attachment before widening court politics
- progression fantasy: volume one may prove the training and cost loop before widening the world
- mystery thriller: volume one may anchor the question chain before enlarging the conspiracy
- political drama: volume one may establish leverage and faction positions before large public moves
- survival horror: volume one may strip safety and trust before expanding the threat map

## Korean Serialization Pressure

When designing for Korean longform or serialized reading, keep these return mechanisms visible:

- a clean opening promise
- chapter-end pressure that feels immediate
- mid-arc reversals that change status or leverage
- volume-end expansion that recontextualizes the next run

## Stop Rule

Stop planning one layer deeper when the current layer no longer changes the shape of the next one. Move to drafting packets only after the first major architecture is stable.
