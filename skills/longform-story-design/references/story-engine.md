# Story Engine

Use this file when the story is still at idea stage or when a premise does not yet seem durable enough for longform scale.

## Build The Engine Before The Lore

Do not start by expanding setting trivia. First prove that the project can keep generating movement.

The engine should answer:

- who keeps pushing forward
- what keeps pushing back
- why the pressure can change without disappearing
- how stakes rise in more than one lane
- what kind of ending promise the opening is aiming toward

## Minimum Durable Engine

Confirm these parts:

- durable protagonist drive: obsession, duty, desire, wound, debt, ambition
- adaptive opposition: person, faction, institution, curse, system, environment
- recurring pressure source: scarcity, hierarchy, taboo, debt, competition, secrecy, survival
- escalation lanes: external, relational, internal, world/system
- return promise: what makes the reader come back after chapter 1, 10, and 40

If one of these is absent, the project may still work, but the expansion plan should stay modest until the gap is repaired.

## Weak Engine Warning Signs

Intervene early if:

- the protagonist can walk away without cost
- the antagonist only blocks once and then repeats
- the setting has flavor but no coercive pressure
- escalation depends only on stronger enemies appearing
- the likely ending has no visible seed in the opening

## Fast Repair Moves

When the engine is weak, repair with the smallest viable change:

- increase obligation instead of adding lore
- add a pressure source that keeps renewing conflict
- split stakes across relational and external lanes
- give opposition its own momentum instead of pure reaction
- narrow the project scale if the engine only supports one contained arc
