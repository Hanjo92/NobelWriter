# Chapter Opening Selection

Use this file when you need a ready-made prompt for the first 3 to 12 lines of a chapter.

## How To Use

1. Pick the genre.
2. Pick the audience tendency.
3. Pick the opening function.
4. Copy the block and replace only scene-specific nouns.

The opening should start movement, pressure, or curiosity fast. Do not spend the first paragraph on lore explanation.

## Core Opening Prompt

```text
Write the opening of a Korean novel chapter.
Genre: [genre]
Audience tendency: [male-oriented / female-oriented / neutral]
Opening function: [hook / confrontation / mystery / emotional aftershock / danger / reunion]
Goal: Make the first lines create immediate forward pressure.
Dialogue rule: If dialogue appears, make it react to the immediately prior line or situation and sound like spoken Korean.
Opening rule: Enter after the change has already begun. Avoid backstory-heavy opening exposition.
```

## Opening Functions

### Hook

```text
Open on a concrete disturbance, revelation, or irreversible decision. Give the reader a question or threat within the first few lines.
```

### Confrontation

```text
Open with clashing intent already in progress. Let the first exchange establish power, injury, or refusal.
```

### Mystery

```text
Open on an anomaly, mismatch, or unexplained trace. Withhold explanation, but make the abnormal detail concrete.
```

### Emotional Aftershock

```text
Open after a fight, confession, betrayal, or failure. Let the first lines show what changed in the relationship or self-understanding.
```

### Danger

```text
Open under active risk. Prioritize orientation, stakes, and the next choice over description.
```

### Reunion

```text
Open at the instant two people meet again under changed circumstances. Let the first beat show what remains unresolved.
```

## Genre Add-Ons

### Traditional Fantasy

```text
Use rank, oath, prophecy, war, or public consequence to weight the opening. Keep diction measured and high-pressure.
```

### Martial Arts

```text
Use sect tension, duel pressure, hidden technique, or reputation threat to sharpen the opening. Keep lines lean and status-aware.
```

### Modern Fantasy

```text
Use anomaly, system notice, urban danger, guild pressure, or hidden rules to hook quickly. Keep the language modern and practical.
```

### Fusion Fantasy

```text
Use mixed-system tension, political leverage, regression knowledge, or strategic imbalance to create immediate intrigue.
```

### Romance

```text
Use attraction, avoidance, unresolved words, or changed relational distance to create pull. Let emotional timing matter as much as information.
```

### Horror

```text
Use an immediate sensory mismatch, breached safe space, missing person trace, or practical survival problem to start dread. Make the abnormal detail concrete before explaining it.
```

Subgenre selectors:

- psychological horror: start with one ordinary detail that becomes privately unbearable or unreliable
- ghost story: start with a returning trace, repeated time, familiar voice, or place memory that should not have returned
- creature horror: start with movement, sound, contamination, or bodily risk already closing in

### Mystery

```text
Use one legible clue, contradiction, or impossible detail to open the chapter. Make the reader ask who, why, or how within the first few lines.
```

Subgenre selectors:

- deduction mystery: open with a clue that breaks the expected logic
- suspense mystery: open with danger tied to missing information or a possible liar
- investigation mystery: open with a report, interview, trace, or institutional mismatch that forces the next step

## Audience Add-Ons

### Male-Oriented

```text
Favor momentum, capability pressure, external stakes, and clean forward motion.
```

### Female-Oriented

```text
Favor relational pressure, emotional interpretation, subtext, and the social meaning of the moment.
```

### Neutral

```text
Balance plot clarity, emotional readability, and immediate accessibility.
```

| Elegant hurt and tension | Traditional fantasy or romance + female-oriented + emotional aftershock | Restraint, subtext, changed distance |
| Urban anomaly opening | Modern fantasy + neutral + mystery | Concrete abnormality, practical response |
| Slow-burn reunion | Romance + neutral or female-oriented + reunion | Unresolved feeling, present reaction |
| Immediate dread in a closed space | Horror + neutral or female-oriented + danger or mystery | Unsafe detail, delayed confirmation |
| Clue-first investigation start | Mystery + male-oriented or neutral + mystery | Contradiction, inference, next question |
| Intimate wrongness from the first line | Horror + psychological + female-oriented + mystery | Unreliable recognition, close unease |
| Haunted return opener | Horror + ghost story + neutral + mystery | Repeated trace, taboo memory |
| Predator-at-the-door opener | Horror + creature horror + male-oriented + danger | Movement, sound, bodily risk |

## Ready-Made Blocks

### Modern Fantasy Neutral Mystery

```text
Write the opening of a Korean novel chapter.
Genre: modern fantasy
Audience tendency: neutral
Opening function: mystery
Goal: Hook the reader with one concrete anomaly and one immediate practical problem.
Dialogue rule: Keep dialogue short, grounded, and reactive.
Opening rule: Do not explain the whole system. Let the abnormal detail lead.
```

### Martial Arts Male-Oriented Confrontation

```text
Write the opening of a Korean novel chapter.
Genre: martial arts
Audience tendency: male-oriented
Opening function: confrontation
Goal: Start with conflict already underway and establish hierarchy fast.
Dialogue rule: Keep exchanges short, sharp, and challenge-driven.
Opening rule: Let reputation, technique, or insult create immediate heat.
```

### Romance Female-Oriented Reunion

```text
Write the opening of a Korean novel chapter.
Genre: romance
Audience tendency: female-oriented
Opening function: reunion
Goal: Make the reunion emotionally loaded from the first glance or first line.
Dialogue rule: Let hesitation, self-protection, and reaction beats shape the exchange.
Opening rule: Show changed distance before explaining history.
```

### Horror Neutral Danger

```text
Write the opening of a Korean novel chapter.
Genre: horror
Audience tendency: neutral
Opening function: danger
Goal: Make one concrete wrong detail turn a familiar space unsafe within the first lines.
Dialogue rule: If dialogue appears, keep it breath-close, reactive, and slightly incomplete under fear.
Opening rule: Start after the unease has already begun. Do not explain the haunting or cause up front.
```

### Mystery Male-Oriented Mystery

```text
Write the opening of a Korean novel chapter.
Genre: mystery
Audience tendency: male-oriented
Opening function: mystery
Goal: Present one clue that immediately breaks the expected explanation.
Dialogue rule: Keep exchanges lean, probing, and tied to observable facts.
Opening rule: Let the contradiction arrive before any backstory.
```

### Psychological Horror Female-Oriented Mystery

```text
Write the opening of a Korean novel chapter.
Genre: horror
Audience tendency: female-oriented
Opening function: mystery
Goal: Make one familiar person, room, or habit feel wrong in a way the narrator notices before they can justify it.
Dialogue rule: If dialogue appears, let it sound careful, doubtful, and slightly self-censored.
Opening rule: Begin with intimate recognition failing in a small but concrete way.
```

### Ghost Story Neutral Mystery

```text
Write the opening of a Korean novel chapter.
Genre: horror
Audience tendency: neutral
Opening function: mystery
Goal: Hook with one returning trace that should have been impossible.
Dialogue rule: Keep any line brief and wary, as if naming the thing gives it weight.
Opening rule: Let the place or object speak first through repetition before explanation arrives.
```

### Creature Horror Male-Oriented Danger

```text
Write the opening of a Korean novel chapter.
Genre: horror
Audience tendency: male-oriented
Opening function: danger
Goal: Start with immediate bodily risk and force a survival choice within the first lines.
Dialogue rule: Keep exchanges clipped, urgent, and action-linked.
Opening rule: Enter after the pursuit or contamination has already begun.
```

### Deduction Mystery Neutral Mystery

```text
Write the opening of a Korean novel chapter.
Genre: mystery
Audience tendency: neutral
Opening function: mystery
Goal: Present one clue whose details quietly invalidate the obvious explanation.
Dialogue rule: Keep dialogue observant and economical.
Opening rule: Show the clue first, then let the question form.
```

### Investigation Mystery Neutral Hook

```text
Write the opening of a Korean novel chapter.
Genre: mystery
Audience tendency: neutral
Opening function: hook
Goal: Use a record, interview, or official trace to create immediate case momentum.
Dialogue rule: Keep exchanges practical and shaped by what can be confirmed now.
Opening rule: Let one documentation gap or testimony mismatch drive the chapter open.
```
