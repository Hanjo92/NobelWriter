# Genre And Audience Samples

Use this reference when the user specifies both a genre direction and a target audience signal such as male-oriented, female-oriented, or neutral.

## How To Use This File

Do not copy these samples verbatim into output unless the user asks for something close to them. Use them to tune:

- opening hook placement
- what the first conflict emphasizes
- how much emotional interiority to surface
- whether dialogue leans blunt, restrained, teasing, or balanced
- what kind of satisfaction the chapter ending should deliver

`male-oriented` here means the story framing tends to foreground upward momentum, capability, tactical gain, and external escalation.

`female-oriented` here means the story framing tends to foreground emotional interpretation, relationship pressure, social texture, and internal stakes alongside plot.

`neutral` here means the story framing should balance plot propulsion, emotional access, and broad readability without leaning too hard into one market pattern.

## Traditional Fantasy

### Male-Oriented

Focus:
- quest momentum
- hierarchy, power, lineage, or hidden potential
- visible external stakes early

Sample setup:
```text
성벽 밖 설원에서 돌아온 전령은 왕의 인장을 품고 죽었다. 문제는 그 인장이 원래 내 것이어야 했다는 점이었다.
```

Sample dialogue:
```text
"지금 문 열면 다 죽습니다."
"안 열면 더 빨리 죽어."
"그럼 네가 먼저 나가."
```

Texture note:
- keep prose direct and forward-driving
- let competence or latent power show through action

### Female-Oriented

Focus:
- court, clan, or oath politics through relational pressure
- destiny filtered through trust, betrayal, and chosen bonds
- emotional cost of duty

Sample setup:
```text
왕궁으로 돌아온 날, 나를 가장 먼저 맞은 것은 개선식이 아니라 약혼 파기의 소문이었다.
```

Sample dialogue:
```text
"축하드려야 하나요, 아니면 위로를 드려야 하나요."
"둘 다 하지 마. 오늘은 웃을 힘도 없으니까."
"그럼 옆에만 있겠습니다."
```

Texture note:
- let emotional reading of status games matter
- let intimacy and distrust share the same scene

### Neutral

Focus:
- adventure plus political stakes
- accessible emotional arc without market-extreme signaling
- ensemble readability

Sample setup:
```text
북방의 봉인이 깨진 밤, 수도에서는 왕위 계승식이 시작됐다. 둘 중 하나만 지켜도 되는 밤은 아니었다.
```

Sample dialogue:
```text
"북문이 먼저 무너집니다."
"확실해?"
"확실하지. 그래서 지금 말하는 거고."
```

Texture note:
- balance spectacle, urgency, and character readability

## Martial Arts

### Male-Oriented

Focus:
- rise through factions and ranks
- clean rivalry, humiliation, comeback, breakthrough
- terse verbal sparring

Sample setup:
```text
파문당한 제자가 산문을 다시 밟은 날, 사형들은 내 이름 대신 배신자라고 불렀다.
```

Sample dialogue:
```text
"네 검은 예전보다 느려졌군."
"죽이기엔 아직 빠르다."
"입은 더 빨라졌네."
```

Texture note:
- keep exchanges sharp and confrontational
- let pride and technique carry the scene

### Female-Oriented

Focus:
- sect politics, reputation, obligation, and relational debt
- strength framed through restraint and reading intent
- dialogue with layered courtesy

Sample setup:
```text
사부님의 부고가 도착한 날, 문파는 애도보다 후계 자리를 먼저 계산하기 시작했다.
```

Sample dialogue:
```text
"조문은 늦으셨네요."
"빈손으로 올 수는 없어서요."
"위로입니까, 거래입니까."
"둘 다 아니라면 믿어 주시겠어요?"
```

Texture note:
- preserve social form while letting knives show underneath

### Neutral

Focus:
- action clarity plus readable emotional stakes
- faction conflict without over-indexing on either swagger or melodrama

Sample setup:
```text
강호에 흉명이 퍼진 살수의 얼굴이, 하필이면 내가 열흘째 함께 걷던 동행과 같았다.
```

Sample dialogue:
```text
"설명할 시간 있어?"
"없지."
"그럼 살아서 해."
```

Texture note:
- keep banter lean and pressure-led

## Modern Fantasy

### Male-Oriented

Focus:
- urban system, hidden power, career or survival advancement
- practical problem-solving under supernatural rules
- clipped dialogue

Sample setup:
```text
각성 통지 문자는 새벽 세 시에 왔다. 출근까지 네 시간, 내 수명은 열이틀 남아 있었다.
```

Sample dialogue:
```text
"이거 진짜 던전 반응 맞아요?"
"가짜면 네 월급으로 보상해 줄게."
"그 말이 제일 무섭거든요."
```

Texture note:
- prioritize hook, rule clarity, and immediate leverage

### Female-Oriented

Focus:
- everyday life rupture plus emotional fallout
- supernatural power entangled with work, family, or chosen relationships
- dialogue that mixes wit with guarded feeling

Sample setup:
```text
퇴사서를 낸 날, 내 그림자에서 낯선 남자가 걸어 나왔다. 문제는 그가 내 이름을 너무 다정하게 불렀다는 것이었다.
```

Sample dialogue:
```text
"저를 아세요?"
"너는 날 몰라도 돼. 아직은."
"그 말, 수상한 거 아시죠."
```

Texture note:
- let mystery and relational charge arrive together

### Neutral

Focus:
- city-scale strangeness with broad readability
- balanced mystery, action, and emotion

Sample setup:
```text
서울 전역의 가로등이 동시에 꺼진 삼 분 동안, 사라진 사람은 스물세 명이었다.
```

Sample dialogue:
```text
"실종자 공통점 찾았어?"
"다 같은 번호로 전화를 받았어."
"그 번호, 지금도 살아 있어?"
```

Texture note:
- keep investigation and feeling in the same channel

## Fusion Fantasy

### Male-Oriented

Focus:
- mixed system progression, empire conflict, tactical growth
- faster payoff and stronger escalation beats

Sample setup:
```text
회귀한 장군의 기억과 마도공학 설계도가 한꺼번에 머릿속에 들어온 순간, 제국의 멸망 연도도 함께 떠올랐다.
```

Sample dialogue:
```text
"지금 그걸 만들 수 있다고?"
"설계는 있다."
"재료는?"
"빼앗으면 되지."
```

Texture note:
- emphasize capability and strategic acceleration

### Female-Oriented

Focus:
- hybrid worldbuilding through status, romance tension, or family duty
- layered emotional stakes inside fast-moving plot

Sample setup:
```text
황실 연회장에서 파혼을 통보받은 직후, 전생의 기억이 돌아왔다. 그리고 눈앞의 마탑주가 내 전생의 원수라는 것도 함께.
```

Sample dialogue:
```text
"지금 웃으시는 건가요?"
"안 웃으면 울 것 같아서요."
"그럼 오늘은 제가 대신 화내 드리죠."
```

Texture note:
- keep emotional reversals crisp and scene-relevant

### Neutral

Focus:
- mixed-genre pace with broad market readability
- political, magical, and personal stakes balanced together

Sample setup:
```text
기계 성좌가 하늘에 떠오른 날, 검성의 제자와 마도학과 조교는 같은 체포 명단에 올랐다.
```

Sample dialogue:
```text
"당신도 쫓기고 있군요."
"듣기 좋은 말은 아니네."
"좋게 들리라고 한 말도 아니고요."
```

Texture note:
- let contrast between systems create energy

## Romance

### Male-Oriented

Focus:
- clear attraction and momentum
- external obstacle tied to competence, status, or danger
- emotionally legible but not overly ornate delivery

Sample setup:
```text
소개팅에 대신 나갔다가, 계약 연애 상대가 하필이면 지난주 구조했던 여자라는 걸 알아버렸다.
```

Sample dialogue:
```text
"지난번 일은 빚으로 쳐요."
"사람 구한 걸요?"
"아뇨. 자꾸 생각나는 거요."
```

Texture note:
- keep attraction direct and scene-active

### Female-Oriented

Focus:
- emotional interpretation, tension, and shifting relational safety
- detailed reaction beats and carefully controlled vulnerability

Sample setup:
```text
결혼식 당일 도망친 건 나였는데, 일주일 뒤 다시 나타난 사람은 그였다. 아무렇지 않은 얼굴로 우산까지 들고서.
```

Sample dialogue:
```text
"왜 왔어요."
"비 오잖아요."
"그걸 핑계라고 해요?"
"핑계라도 있어야 네 앞에 서지."
```

Texture note:
- let longing, pride, and hesitation coexist in the same exchange

### Neutral

Focus:
- balanced chemistry, banter, and emotional honesty
- accessible pacing across different reader expectations

Sample setup:
```text
이사 첫날 옆집 남자와 최악의 방식으로 마주쳤다. 내 택배를 들고 웃고 있었으니까.
```

Sample dialogue:
```text
"남의 택배는 왜 들고 있어요?"
"엘리베이터 문에 끼일 뻔해서요."
"그래도 수상하거든요."
"그건 인정할게요."
```

Texture note:
- keep charm and plausibility in balance

## Horror

### Subgenre Split

Use this before choosing audience tone:

| Subgenre | Core pressure | Best default texture |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Psychological horror | doubt, guilt, perception failure, intimate wrongness | inward dread, unstable certainty, small details turning hostile |
| Ghost story | return, unresolved dead, place memory, ritual breach | lingering atmosphere, recognition, old promise or grievance |
| Creature horror | pursuit, contamination, body risk, survival decisions | direct sensory threat, kinetic fear, rapid practical response |

### Male-Oriented

Focus:
- immediate physical unease or survival pressure
- visible threat, contaminated space, or escalating wrongness
- short decision-driven beats under fear

Sample setup:
```text
문을 연 건 서른 초쯤 지난 뒤였다. 그동안 안에서는 아무 소리도 안 났는데, 막상 열리자 누가 방금까지 숨을 참고 서 있던 것 같은 공기가 밀려 나왔다.
```

Sample dialogue:
```text
"방 안에 누구 있었어?"
"없었어. 근데 문이 안에서 잠겨 있었잖아."
"그러니까 더 빨리 나가자고."
```

Texture note:
- keep fear practical before poetic
- let action choices sharpen the dread

### Female-Oriented

Focus:
- dread through relationship fracture, memory, or violated safety
- unease that begins in ordinary care and turns intimate
- emotional reading of what feels wrong before proof arrives

Sample setup:
```text
동생은 분명 웃고 있었는데, 서윤은 그 웃는 얼굴이 자기 기억 속 얼굴이 아니라는 걸 먼저 알아봤다. 입꼬리는 같았지만, 기다리는 방식이 달랐다.
```

Sample dialogue:
```text
"너, 아까부터 왜 자꾸 나를 모르는 사람처럼 봐?"
"내가?"
"응. 네가 원래 그런 눈으로는 안 보거든."
```

Texture note:
- let safety turn strange by degrees
- keep dread close to attachment and recognition

### Neutral

Focus:
- concrete anomaly plus readable emotional tension
- investigation and fear advancing together
- broad readability without flattening atmosphere

Sample setup:
```text
관리실 기록에는 분명 새벽 두 시 십삼 분에 엘리베이터가 십사 층에 멈춘 걸로 찍혀 있었다. 문제는 그 층이 세 달 전에 막혀 버렸다는 점이었다.
```

Sample dialogue:
```text
"기록 오류 아냐?"
"오류면 문이 왜 열려 있어."
"그 말은, 누가 있었다는 거네."
```

Texture note:
- anchor dread in a checkable fact
- balance atmosphere with forward motion

### Horror Subgenre Notes

#### Psychological Horror

Use when:
- the main fear comes from perception, memory, or self-doubt
- the threat may be real, misread, or both

Keep:
- repeated ordinary details that turn unreliable
- dialogue that hesitates because the speaker fears sounding irrational

#### Ghost Story

Use when:
- the fear is tied to a place, a dead person, or an unfinished promise
- the uncanny feels familiar before it feels dangerous

Keep:
- traces, routines, objects, names, or times that return
- dialogue that mixes recognition, taboo, and half-belief

#### Creature Horror

Use when:
- the fear is embodied, hunting, spreading, or physically immediate
- survival action matters as much as atmosphere

Keep:
- concrete motion, sound, smell, and injury risk
- dialogue that prioritizes warning, command, and quick denial

## Mystery

### Subgenre Split

Use this before choosing audience tone:

| Subgenre | Core pressure | Best default texture |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Deduction mystery | impossible clue, contradiction, inference race | crisp clue logic, interpretive confidence, controlled reveal |
| Suspense mystery | who is lying, who is in danger, what will happen next | pressure, withheld motive, unstable trust |
| Investigation mystery | case progression, interviews, records, institutional friction | procedural clarity, layered testimony, clue accumulation |

### Male-Oriented

Focus:
- puzzle, leverage, and information asymmetry
- fast inference under pressure
- dialogue that tests facts and motives efficiently

Sample setup:
```text
피해자는 넘어지기 전에 이미 신발을 벗고 있었다. 도망치던 사람의 자세가 아니었다는 뜻이었다.
```

Sample dialogue:
```text
"도망치다 넘어진 게 아니네."
"왜 그렇게 봐요?"
"급한 사람은 신발부터 벗지 않아."
```

Texture note:
- prioritize clean clue logic
- let competence and pressure share the page

### Female-Oriented

Focus:
- hidden motive, relational mismatch, and social reading
- tension from what people protect as much as what they lie about
- emotional undercurrent without losing clue discipline

Sample setup:
```text
장례식장에 온 사람들은 다 울었는데, 막내며느리만 너무 정확한 타이밍에만 울었다. 희진은 그게 오히려 더 수상하다고 느꼈다.
```

Sample dialogue:
```text
"슬퍼 보이질 않아서가 아니에요."
"그럼 뭐가 이상한데요."
"너무 준비된 사람 같았어요."
```

Texture note:
- let motive-reading sharpen the mystery
- keep feeling tied to observable behavior

### Neutral

Focus:
- clue progression, human motive, and accessible tension
- questions that widen the case while staying readable
- balance deduction and emotional stakes

Sample setup:
```text
사라진 사람의 휴대전화는 강가에서 발견됐다. 그런데 마지막 통화 기록은 강가가 아니라, 사건을 담당한 형사의 개인 번호로 남아 있었다.
```

Sample dialogue:
```text
"이건 설명이 필요하겠네요."
"저도 그 말을 하려고 했습니다."
"그럼 지금부터는 둘 중 하나예요. 실수였거나, 숨긴 거거나."
```

Texture note:
- keep each clue legible and consequential
- let suspicion move through both fact and relationship

### Mystery Subgenre Notes

#### Deduction Mystery

Use when:
- the hook is a contradiction, impossibility, or elegant clue break
- solving the logic matters more than chase energy

Keep:
- observable detail first, interpretation second
- dialogue that tests theories by fact, timing, and exception

#### Suspense Mystery

Use when:
- the mystery is tied to immediate danger, betrayal, or hidden intent
- the reader should feel threat before full explanation

Keep:
- partial information, pressure, and motive-reading
- dialogue that circles, probes, and conceals

#### Investigation Mystery

Use when:
- the story advances through interviews, reports, traces, and reconstruction
- institutions, procedure, or hierarchy affect what can be learned

Keep:
- clue chains the reader can track
- dialogue that separates testimony, interpretation, and omission
