# Martial Arts Starter Sets

Use this file when the user wants a chapter 1 start for martial arts fiction.

## Audience Tendency Modifiers

### Male-Oriented

- emphasize humiliation, rivalry, ascent, and breakthrough pressure
- keep exchanges short, challenging, and action-linked

### Female-Oriented

- emphasize sect etiquette, relational debt, hidden faction strain, and restrained hurt
- let courtesy and emotional reading share the same scene

### Neutral

- balance action clarity, faction pressure, and readable character tension
- keep jianghu texture strong without over-leaning into swagger or melodrama

## Quick Variant Blocks

Use these after choosing one of the starter types below.

### Male-Oriented Block

```text
Audience tendency: male-oriented
Emphasis: rivalry, status clash, humiliation, comeback pressure
Rule: Keep the opening sharp and kinetic. Let challenge arrive early.
```

### Female-Oriented Block

```text

Rule: Keep the opening restrained on the surface and dangerous underneath.
```

### Neutral Block

```text
Audience tendency: neutral
Emphasis: sect tension, readable stakes, balanced pace
Rule: Let action and social pressure support each other without excess.
```

## Rise-Through-Sect

Core promise:
- humiliation, rank pressure, and breakthrough momentum begin early

Best protagonist position:
- a low-ranked disciple, outcast student, or overlooked outer-court fighter

Opening patterns:
- public test or ranking match
- punishment assignment that becomes opportunity
- open mockery before a skill or willpower reveal

First chapter checklist:
- one hierarchy marker
- one insult, slight, or visible disadvantage
- one immediate chance to act
- one sign the protagonist is not as weak as assumed

Avoid:
- delaying the first hierarchy clash
- making the protagonist passive too long
- explaining sect structure in blocks

Micro sample:
```text
연무장 모래판은 아직 젖어 있었는데, 사형들은 이미 진 사람 이름부터 부르고 있었다. 준혁은 자기 이름이 그 명단 맨 앞에 오른 걸 보고서야 오늘이 단순한 시합이 아니라는 걸 알았다.
```

Draft block:
```text
Write the first scene of a Korean martial arts rise-through-sect story.
Goal: Start with hierarchy pressure, humiliation, and one immediate chance to answer back through action.
Checklist: Include one status marker, one slight, one action opening, and one hint of hidden capability.
Dialogue rule: Keep exchanges short, sharp, and sect-aware.
Opening rule: Begin inside the clash, not before it.
```

Revision block:
```text
Revise this chapter opening as Korean martial arts rise fiction.
Goal: Increase rivalry heat and forward drive.
Rule: Cut setup that delays the challenge. Let insult, status, and action pressure lead.
```

## Sect Intrigue

Core promise:
- courtesy hides knives and politics arrives through ritual or obligation

Best protagonist position:
- an inner disciple, envoy, healer, or adopted heir caught between factions

Opening patterns:
- a formal reception turns into a test
- an elder's instruction conflicts with sect loyalty
- a marriage, alliance, or inheritance talk carries hidden threat

First chapter checklist:
- one formal courtesy exchange
- one hidden faction signal
- one obligation the protagonist cannot shrug off
- one clue that the conflict is larger than the room

Avoid:
- flattening intrigue into simple good-versus-evil
- dropping too many faction names too fast
- forgetting body language and etiquette

Micro sample:
```text
상객에게 올리는 첫 잔은 오른손으로만 받는다. 그런데 청운문의 둘째 장로는 잔을 받기 전에 먼저 은서를 본 뒤, 아주 짧게 왼손 소매를 눌렀다. 예법을 아는 사람이라면 더더욱 하지 않을 동작이었다.
```

Draft block:
```text
Write the first scene of a Korean martial arts sect-intrigue story.
Goal: Open on courtesy carrying faction pressure.
Checklist: Include one ritualized exchange, one hidden signal, one obligation, and one wider political implication.
Dialogue rule: Let politeness and threat coexist.
Opening rule: Show the signal before explaining the feud.
```

Revision block:
```text
Revise this chapter opening as Korean martial arts intrigue.
Goal: Sharpen subtext without losing readability.
Rule: Keep etiquette visible, but make the hidden threat legible through gesture, pause, and wording choice.
```

## Wandering Jianghu

Core promise:
- the road itself generates trouble, debt, and reputation

Best protagonist position:
- a drifter, guard-for-hire, runaway disciple, or swordsman with unfinished business

Opening patterns:
- roadside inn trouble
- escort job gone wrong
- a stranger recognizes a name the protagonist meant to bury

First chapter checklist:
- one transient setting
- one stranger or rumor
- one threat tied to reputation
- one reason the protagonist must get involved instead of moving on

Avoid:
- treating the road as empty filler
- making the protagonist too detached to hook
- replacing scene conflict with travel summary

Micro sample:
```text
주막 주인은 칼값보다 먼저 말값을 물었다. 서린은 그 말이 이상하다고 느끼기도 전에, 구석에서 국수를 먹던 사내 하나가 웃으며 제 아버지의 호를 입에 올렸다.
```

Draft block:
```text
Write the first scene of a Korean wandering-jianghu martial arts story.
Goal: Use a roadside setting to trigger immediate trouble through rumor, debt, or buried reputation.
Checklist: Include one transient place, one stranger, one reputation threat, and one reason the protagonist must stay.
Dialogue rule: Keep speech lean, wary, and ready to turn.
Opening rule: Let the trouble arrive before travel exposition.
```

Revision block:
```text
Revise this chapter opening as Korean wandering-jianghu fiction.
Goal: Increase road energy and lived jianghu texture.
Rule: Replace generic travel mood with one concrete place detail and one immediate interpersonal threat.
```
