# Modern Fantasy Starter Sets

Use this file when the user wants a chapter 1 start for modern fantasy.

## Audience Tendency Modifiers

### Male-Oriented

- emphasize leverage, survival advantage, career or combat pressure, and quick payoff
- keep the opening practical, clipped, and consequence-first

### Female-Oriented

- emphasize rupture inside work, family, or chosen relationships
- let anomaly and emotional fallout arrive together

### Neutral

- balance city-scale strangeness, practical stakes, and emotional readability
- keep the anomaly clear without overexplaining the system

## Quick Variant Blocks

Use these after choosing one of the starter types below.

### Male-Oriented Block

```text
Audience tendency: male-oriented

```

### Female-Oriented Block


## Awakening And System

Core promise:
- ordinary life breaks because the protagonist can no longer ignore hidden rules

Best protagonist position:
- a worker, student, or exhausted adult already under practical pressure when the anomaly hits

Opening patterns:
- a system message arrives during mundane stress
- a monster or ability manifests in a routine place
- a survival problem becomes solvable only through hidden rules

First chapter checklist:
- one practical life problem
- one visible anomaly
- one immediate benefit or danger
- one reason the protagonist has to engage now

Avoid:
- explaining the entire system in the first scene
- making the anomaly feel detached from daily life
- relying on stat readout instead of scene tension

Micro sample:
```text
민재는 출근 지각보다 카드값부터 생각하고 있었다. 그래서 횡단보도 위에 떠오른 반투명 창이 처음엔 광고인 줄 알았다. 문제는 그 창이, 바로 다음 신호에서 누가 죽는다고 너무 정확하게 적고 있었다는 점이었다.
```

Draft block:
```text
Write the first scene of a Korean awakening-and-system modern fantasy story.
Goal: Break ordinary life with one anomaly that creates immediate risk or leverage.
Checklist: Include one mundane pressure, one anomaly, one concrete consequence, and one forced next action.
Dialogue rule: Keep dialogue practical, surprised, and rooted in the current problem.
Opening rule: Let the anomaly interrupt life in progress.
```

Revision block:
```text
Revise this chapter opening as Korean awakening modern fantasy.
Goal: Strengthen the collision between ordinary stress and hidden rules.
Rule: Cut system explanation that does not affect the immediate choice. Let scene pressure carry the hook.
```

## Hidden City Anomaly

Core promise:
- the familiar city contains a second logic that suddenly becomes visible

Best protagonist position:
- a dispatcher, journalist, civil servant, cab driver, or witness used to the city's normal flow

Opening patterns:
- infrastructure record or CCTV shows the impossible
- a routine route contains an unlisted place
- a city worker meets a problem no manual explains

First chapter checklist:
- one urban routine
- one impossible trace
- one institutional or practical response
- one sign the city has been hiding this for a while

Avoid:
- using generic city description without an anomaly
- treating the city as backdrop rather than engine
- moving too fast into explanation

Micro sample:
```text
택시 앱에는 없는 골목이 내비게이션에는 세 번이나 떴다. 유나는 오작동이라고 넘기려다가, 그 길로 들어간 손님들의 결제가 단 한 건도 끝까지 찍히지 않았다는 걸 보고 핸들을 놓지 못했다.
```

Draft block:
```text
Write the first scene of a Korean hidden-city modern fantasy story.
Goal: Reveal one urban anomaly through a routine system, map, record, or route.
Checklist: Include one city routine, one impossible trace, one practical response, and one sign this anomaly is not new.
Dialogue rule: Keep speech grounded in work, movement, and immediate verification.
Opening rule: Let the city glitch before the lore arrives.
```

Revision block:
```text
Revise this chapter opening as Korean hidden-city modern fantasy.
Goal: Increase urban specificity and anomaly clarity.
Rule: Replace generic strangeness with one concrete city system or record that fails in a meaningful way.
```

## Everyday Rupture

Core promise:
- family, work, or friendship is destabilized because the supernatural is now personal

Best protagonist position:
- someone whose emotional life is already strained before the uncanny enters

Opening patterns:
- a loved one behaves with impossible knowledge
- a home object, photo, or message changes
- a minor domestic scene is interrupted by a supernatural detail only one person notices

First chapter checklist:
- one close relationship
- one uncanny break inside ordinary life
- one emotional cost
- one reason the protagonist cannot dismiss it

Avoid:
- making the domestic setup too static
- jumping to melodrama before the uncanny lands
- using dialogue as explanation

Micro sample:
```text
동생은 분명 야근 중이어야 했다. 그런데 식탁 위에 식어 가는 국은 두 사람 몫으로 놓여 있었고, 메모지에는 오늘은 늦지 말라는 동생 글씨가 적혀 있었다. 문제는 그 글씨가, 동생이 다친 뒤로는 다시는 쓸 수 없던 쪽손이었다.
```

Draft block:
```text
Write the first scene of a Korean everyday-rupture modern fantasy story.
Goal: Let one domestic or relational moment break because of a supernatural detail.
Checklist: Include one close relationship, one uncanny break, one emotional cost, and one reason dismissal is impossible.
Dialogue rule: Keep speech natural, intimate, and slightly defensive.
Opening rule: Start inside the ordinary moment before the break becomes undeniable.
```

Revision block:
```text
Revise this chapter opening as Korean everyday-rupture modern fantasy.
Goal: Increase emotional immediacy without losing the anomaly.
Rule: Keep the supernatural tied to a relationship beat. Cut any abstract explanation that weakens the scene.
```
