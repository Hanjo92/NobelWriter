# Mystery Starter Sets

Use this file when the user wants a chapter 1 start for mystery.

## Audience Tendency Modifiers

### Male-Oriented

- emphasize clean contradiction, inference pressure, and tactical information gain
- keep the opening concise and clue-led

### Female-Oriented

- emphasize hidden motive, social reading, unstable trust, and emotional mismatch
- let suspicion move through behavior as much as evidence

### Neutral

- balance clue clarity, human motive, and accessible tension
- keep both puzzle and emotional stakes readable

## Quick Variant Blocks

Use these after choosing one of the starter types below.

### Male-Oriented Block

```text
Audience tendency: male-oriented
Emphasis: contradiction, inference, fast clue pressure
Rule: Let the opening move from observable fact to strategic question quickly.
```

### Female-Oriented Block

```text
Audience tendency: female-oriented
Emphasis: motive-reading, unstable trust, social tension
Rule: Keep suspicion tied to behavior, timing, and relational mismatch.
```

### Neutral Block

```text
Audience tendency: neutral
Emphasis: clue legibility, human motive, broad readability
Rule: Let the opening raise a clean question while keeping people central.
```

## Deduction Mystery

Core promise:
- one clue quietly breaks the obvious explanation

Best protagonist position:
- someone who notices what others treat as noise
- a thinker whose first advantage is interpretation, not force

Opening patterns:
- a small physical detail invalidates the accepted story
- a witness statement is technically true but structurally wrong
- an impossible sequence appears in a mundane setting

First chapter checklist:
- one legible clue
- one default explanation
- one contradiction that breaks it
- one next question or next check

Avoid:
- dumping the full solution logic too early
- using a clue the reader cannot visualize
- making everyone except the protagonist foolish

Micro sample:
```text
넘어진 사람 옆에 깨진 안경이 있었지만, 렌즈에는 빗방울이 한 점도 없었다. 비를 맞고 쓰러진 사람치고는 이상하리만치 늦게 바닥에 놓인 물건 같았다.
```

Draft block:
```text
Write the first scene of a Korean deduction mystery.
Audience tendency: [neutral / male-oriented]
Goal: Present one clue that quietly invalidates the obvious explanation.
Checklist: Include one visible clue, one mistaken assumption, one contradiction, and one next investigative move.
Dialogue rule: Let dialogue move from observation to tentative inference without sounding like a lecture.
Opening rule: Show the clue before the theory.
```

Revision block:
```text
Revise this chapter opening as a Korean deduction mystery.
Goal: Sharpen clue legibility and inferential tension.
Rule: Make the contradiction cleaner and earlier. Remove any explanatory line that tells the reader what the clue already shows.
```

## Suspense Mystery

Core promise:
- the missing truth is dangerous because someone is lying, hiding, or too late

Best protagonist position:
- someone close enough to the danger to be affected before they understand it
- someone who must read people, not just evidence

Opening patterns:
- a conversation is normal on the surface but wrong underneath
- a warning arrives too late or too vaguely
- an ally behaves in a way that shifts trust immediately

First chapter checklist:
- one unstable relationship
- one withheld fact
- one pressure source or immediate danger
- one reason the protagonist cannot step back

Avoid:
- making the scene mysterious only because information is arbitrarily withheld from the reader
- replacing pressure with vague foreboding
- overexplaining suspicion instead of dramatizing it

Micro sample:
```text
형부는 들어오자마자 현관 불부터 껐다. 손에 짐도 있었고, 비까지 맞었는데 굳이 그 순서를 먼저 고른 게 이상했다. 더 이상한 건, 언니가 그걸 보고도 아무 말도 하지 않았다는 점이었다.
```

Draft block:
```text
Write the first scene of a Korean suspense mystery.
Audience tendency: [female-oriented / neutral]
Goal: Create danger through unstable trust and one withheld fact.
Checklist: Include one suspicious behavior, one strained relationship beat, one immediate risk, and one reason the protagonist must stay engaged.
Dialogue rule: Let dialogue conceal as much as it reveals. Keep each line socially plausible.
Opening rule: Make the pressure felt before the theory is formed.
```

Revision block:
```text
Revise this chapter opening as a Korean suspense mystery.
Goal: Increase mistrust and immediate pressure without becoming melodramatic.
Rule: Let danger travel through behavior, timing, and omission. Cut any sentence that explains suspicion more directly than the scene needs.
```

## Investigation Mystery

Core promise:
- the case advances through records, testimony, and procedural friction

Best protagonist position:
- a 조사자, 기자, 기록 담당자, or reluctant insider with access to partial information
- someone constrained by rules, hierarchy, or missing documents

Opening patterns:
- a report and a witness account do not match
- a case file contains a small but costly omission
- an interview produces one answer that creates a larger procedural problem

First chapter checklist:
- one document, trace, or testimony
- one mismatch between sources
- one institutional limit or friction
- one next procedural action

Avoid:
- front-loading too many case facts without a usable contradiction
- writing procedure without human tension
- making the investigation feel like summary rather than active scene work

Micro sample:
```text
실종 신고서에는 보호자가 자정 전에 귀가하지 않았다고 적혀 있었지만, 편의점 정산 기록에는 새벽 한 시 십이 분에 그 사람의 사번이 남아 있었다. 기록이 맞다면 신고 시각부터가 거짓이었다.
```

Draft block:
```text
Write the first scene of a Korean investigation mystery.
Audience tendency: [neutral]
Goal: Launch the case through one document or testimony mismatch that forces the next step.
Checklist: Include one record, one contradiction, one institutional friction point, and one actionable follow-up.
Dialogue rule: Keep exchanges precise, procedural, and naturally spoken.
Opening rule: Let the case pressure begin through something verifiable.
```

Revision block:
```text
Revise this chapter opening as a Korean investigation mystery.
Goal: Improve procedural clarity and momentum.
Rule: Make the mismatch easier to grasp on first read and ensure the scene ends with a concrete next move.
```
