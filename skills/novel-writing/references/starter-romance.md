# Romance Starter Sets

Use this file when the user wants a chapter 1 start for romance.

## Audience Tendency Modifiers

### Male-Oriented

- emphasize clear attraction, scene-active momentum, and external obstacle
- keep emotional movement legible but not over-ornamented

### Female-Oriented

- emphasize emotional interpretation, shifting safety, hesitation, and relational subtext
- let reaction beats carry as much weight as information

### Neutral

- balance chemistry, banter, and emotional honesty
- keep the opening accessible without draining tension

## Quick Variant Blocks

Use these after choosing one of the starter types below.

### Male-Oriented Block

```text
Audience tendency: male-oriented
Emphasis: direct pull, scene momentum, obstacle-linked attraction
Rule: Keep chemistry active and tied to what the characters must do now.
```

### Female-Oriented Block

```text

Emphasis: hesitation, subtext, changing relational safety
Rule: Let attraction emerge through reaction, avoidance, and emotional reading.
```

### Neutral Block

```text
Audience tendency: neutral
Emphasis: balanced chemistry, banter, and clear feeling
Rule: Keep the opening warm, plausible, and emotionally readable.
```

## First Encounter

Core promise:
- attraction begins through conflict, misread, or badly timed recognition

Best protagonist position:
- someone already preoccupied with another problem when the other lead enters

Opening patterns:
- first meeting through interference or inconvenience
- one person sees the other at their least prepared moment
- a practical problem forces repeated contact immediately

First chapter checklist:
- one spark or strong read
- one obstacle to easy liking
- one reason they must stay in contact
- one emotionally memorable image or line

Avoid:
- using only physical description
- making the attraction instantly mutual and fully articulated
- stopping the scene for backstory

Micro sample:
```text
우산은 하나였고, 사과는 세 번이나 엇나갔다. 다정하다고 느끼기엔 상대가 너무 재수 없었고, 무례하다고 넘기기엔 마지막 한마디가 이상하게 오래 남았다.
```

Draft block:
```text
Write the first scene of a Korean romance first-encounter story.
Goal: Start attraction through conflict, misread, or inconvenient contact.
Checklist: Include one spark, one obstacle, one reason for continued contact, and one memorable emotional detail.
Dialogue rule: Keep speech reactive, natural, and slightly edged by self-protection.
Opening rule: Enter at the collision point, not before it.
```

Revision block:
```text
Revise this chapter opening as Korean romance first encounter.
Goal: Increase chemistry without flattening conflict.
Rule: Let attraction appear through reaction, timing, and remembered details rather than explicit summary.
```

## Reunion Romance

Core promise:
- changed distance and unfinished feeling are visible from the first glance

Best protagonist position:
- someone who thought the relationship was over, settled, or safely buried

Opening patterns:
- unexpected reunion in a professional or family setting
- one person returns changed, the other notices first
- a practical reason prevents either person from leaving

First chapter checklist:
- one unresolved past
- one changed present detail
- one emotional misfire or restraint
- one reason the reunion cannot be brief

Avoid:
- explaining the entire breakup or history up front
- making both leads immediately confessional
- skipping the changed-distance beat

Micro sample:
```text
이름표를 보기 전까지는 못 알아볼 줄 알았다. 그런데 수아는 얼굴보다 먼저 컵을 잡는 손에서 그 사람을 알아봤다. 예전보다 훨씬 조용해졌는데, 그래서 더 늦었다.
```

Draft block:
```text
Write the first scene of a Korean reunion romance.
Goal: Make changed distance and unfinished feeling visible from the first beat.
Checklist: Include one past tension, one changed present detail, one restrained reaction, and one reason they must remain together.
Dialogue rule: Let hesitation and emotional cover shape the lines.
Opening rule: Show changed distance before history explanation.
```

Revision block:
```text
Revise this chapter opening as Korean reunion romance.
Goal: Strengthen emotional charge without overexplaining the past.
Rule: Keep one visible change and one restrained reaction at the center. Let history leak in through what is noticed and avoided.
```

## Forced Proximity

Core promise:
- two people who would prefer distance must share space, task, or time

Best protagonist position:
- coworkers, contract partners, neighbors, housemates, or teammates with immediate friction

Opening patterns:
- contract, assignment, or housing problem forces closeness
- one boundary is set and immediately tested
- a practical arrangement reveals emotional incompatibility

First chapter checklist:
- one reason they are stuck together
- one visible incompatibility
- one shared task or space
- one glimpse that the dynamic may flip later

Avoid:
- making the setup feel too contrived without practical logic
- rushing into tenderness
- using dialogue to state the trope

Micro sample:
```text
관리실은 사흘 안에 누수 공사를 끝내겠다고 했고, 그 말은 곧 주방을 같이 써야 한다는 뜻이었다. 연우는 그 사실보다, 맞은편 남자가 자기 컵 받침을 이미 제 자리에서 치워 둔 걸 먼저 문제라고 느꼈다.
```

Draft block:
```text
Write the first scene of a Korean forced-proximity romance.
Goal: Set the shared space or task that neither lead can easily avoid.
Checklist: Include one practical lock, one incompatibility, one shared environment detail, and one hint of later reversal.
Dialogue rule: Keep the tension speakable and specific, not trope-labeled.
Opening rule: Let the logistical trap appear early.
```

Revision block:
```text
Revise this chapter opening as Korean forced-proximity romance.
Goal: Sharpen friction and chemistry at the same time.
Rule: Keep the practical setup believable and let attraction remain one beat behind annoyance.
```
