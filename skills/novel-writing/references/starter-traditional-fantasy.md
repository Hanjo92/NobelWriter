# Traditional Fantasy Starter Sets

Use this file when the user wants a chapter 1 start for traditional fantasy.

## Audience Tendency Modifiers

### Male-Oriented

- emphasize quest momentum, public consequence, and visible capability pressure
- keep emotion present, but subordinate it to duty, danger, and forward motion

### Female-Oriented

- emphasize oath pressure, relational loyalty, betrayal risk, and emotional reading of hierarchy
- let trust, distance, and chosen bonds change the weight of the opening

### Neutral

- balance adventure pull, court or road pressure, and readable emotional stakes
- keep the opening accessible without flattening rank or consequence

## Quick Variant Blocks

Use these after choosing one of the starter types below.

### Male-Oriented Block

```text
Audience tendency: male-oriented
Emphasis: forward motion, visible consequence, competence under pressure
Rule: Keep the opening direct and scene-active. Let status and danger sharpen action.
```

### Female-Oriented Block

```text
Audience tendency: female-oriented
Emphasis: relational stakes, loyalty pressure, emotional interpretation of rank
Rule: Let the opening carry bond tension and social cost alongside the external disturbance.
```

### Neutral Block

```text
Audience tendency: neutral
Emphasis: balanced adventure, consequence, and readable feeling
Rule: Keep both plot pressure and emotional access clear on first read.
```

## Quest Fantasy

Core promise:
- the protagonist is pulled toward a larger road, duty, or relic they cannot ignore

Best protagonist position:
- a reluctant heir, scout, squire, or low-ranking witness near the first disturbance

Opening patterns:
- a messenger arrives too late
- a relic reacts in public
- a local disaster reveals a larger summons

First chapter checklist:
- one disturbance with public consequence
- one concrete call to motion
- one rank or duty pressure
- one reason refusal will cost something

Avoid:
- opening with lore summary
- delaying movement until chapter 2
- using grand language before stakes are concrete

Micro sample:
```text
봉화가 오른 건 동이 트기 직전이었다. 북문 초소 하나쯤 타는 일로는 종을 세 번이나 울리지 않는다. 이안은 그 소리를 듣자마자, 성벽 위가 아니라 자기 집 문 앞부터 떠올렸다. 아버지가 돌아오지 않았기 때문이다.
```

Draft block:
```text
Write the first scene of a Korean traditional fantasy quest story.
Goal: Start with a public disturbance that forces the protagonist toward a larger road, duty, or relic.
Checklist: Include one visible consequence, one movement-trigger, one hierarchy pressure, and one personal reason the protagonist cannot stay still.
Dialogue rule: Let rank and urgency shape the speech without turning theatrical.
Opening rule: Begin after the disturbance has already started.
```

Revision block:
```text
Revise this chapter opening as Korean traditional fantasy quest fiction.
Goal: Increase forward pull and public consequence.
Rule: Cut lore explanation that does not change the protagonist's immediate decision. Let rank, alarm, and motion carry the opening.
```

## Court Fantasy

Core promise:
- power shifts before the protagonist fully knows the price

Best protagonist position:
- a minor noble, attendant, scribe, hostage, or sworn retainer close to the center but not in control

Opening patterns:
- a ceremonial moment breaks in a small but dangerous way
- a title is given to the wrong person
- a private instruction contradicts the public script

First chapter checklist:
- one formal setting
- one breach of decorum
- one power mismatch
- one private risk hidden inside public manners

Avoid:
- explaining the entire succession map at once
- writing everyone with the same stately tone
- letting politics stay abstract

Micro sample:
```text
왕비는 축복문을 읽는 동안 단 한 번도 세자의 쪽을 보지 않았다. 대신 이름도 불리지 않은 셋째 왕자를 향해 두 번 고개를 기울였다. 그 짧은 각도만으로도 연회장은 이미 늦었다고 윤서는 알아봤다.
```

Draft block:
```text
Write the first scene of a Korean traditional fantasy court story.
Goal: Open on public decorum breaking in a way that immediately alters power.
Checklist: Include one formal ritual, one breach, one hidden personal risk, and one new question about loyalty or succession.
Dialogue rule: Keep speech rank-aware, restrained, and socially costly.
Opening rule: Let the breach arrive before the full political explanation.
```

Revision block:
```text
Revise this chapter opening as Korean traditional fantasy court fiction.
Goal: Sharpen status pressure and subtext.
Rule: Keep the public face controlled, but make the breach and its consequence legible within the scene.
```

## Ruin And Relic Fantasy

Core promise:
- the past becomes active through one place, object, or sealed memory

Best protagonist position:
- a guide, archivist, junior mage, grave-keeper, or expedition member near forbidden ground

Opening patterns:
- a seal cracks during routine work
- an excavation reveals an impossible emblem
- an old ruin responds to the wrong person

First chapter checklist:
- one ancient trace
- one present-day practical problem
- one sign of recognition between past and protagonist
- one reason the site cannot be abandoned

Avoid:
- using only atmosphere without a concrete trigger
- overdescribing the ruin before the threat or pull lands
- explaining the relic's full legend immediately

Micro sample:
```text
석실 문양은 어제까지도 죽은 돌 같았다. 그런데 오늘은 하준이 횃불을 들자, 가장 안쪽 금선 하나만 젖은 뱀처럼 번들거렸다. 대장이 욕을 삼킨 건 그다음이었다. 그 문양이 하준의 가문 인장과 같았기 때문이다.
```

Draft block:
```text
Write the first scene of a Korean traditional fantasy ruin-and-relic story.
Goal: Make one ancient place or object react in a way that ties the past to the protagonist.
Checklist: Include one physical trace, one practical obstacle, one uncanny recognition, and one reason the expedition cannot simply retreat.
Dialogue rule: Keep speech functional under discovery pressure, with rank and caution intact.
Opening rule: Show the reaction first, then widen to the legend later.
```

Revision block:
```text
Revise this chapter opening as Korean traditional fantasy ruin fiction.
Goal: Increase the sense that the past has become active now.
Rule: Keep one concrete relic response at the center and cut decorative history that is not yet needed.
```
