# Failure Patterns

Use this file when the manuscript feels wrong but the cause is not yet named clearly.

## Start With Common Failures

Use this file first for cross-genre failures that damage most long-form fiction regardless of category.

## Then Route By Genre Promise

If the project has a strong genre engine, also read the matching genre file:

- [romance-failures.md](romance-failures.md) for relationship-driven romance, romantic fantasy, and romance-heavy serials
- [mystery-failures.md](mystery-failures.md) for mystery, thriller, investigation, and secret-driven suspense
- [progression-fantasy-failures.md](progression-fantasy-failures.md) for progression fantasy, training arcs, rank-up stories, and power-growth web novels
- [political-intrigue-failures.md](political-intrigue-failures.md) for court drama, faction conflict, succession stories, governance conflict, and strategy-heavy intrigue

Use genre-specific files to check whether the manuscript is breaking its main reader promise, not just its general structure.

## Common Long-Form Failure Modes

### Story Engine Fatigue

Symptoms:

- the same type of obstacle returns without new pressure
- chapters move but the promise does not deepen
- side complications replace core momentum

Likely cause:

- the protagonist's desire cannot sustain the scale
- opposition evolves too slowly

### Escalation Without Cost

Symptoms:

- bigger events happen, but they feel lighter
- power growth or plot expansion does not demand sacrifice

Likely cause:

- consequences are delayed, reversible, or abstract

### Relationship Reset

Symptoms:

- emotional progress disappears after each arc
- conflict beats repeat with new wording only

Likely cause:

- status quo is being restored to preserve the engine
- no permanent relational price is attached to turning points

### Continuity Drift

Symptoms:

- characters know things too early
- injuries or objects disappear
- rules flex only when convenient

Likely cause:

- drafting advanced faster than the tracking layer

### Promise-Payoff Breakdown

Symptoms:

- important setup never returns
- reveals land with little force
- climactic turns feel imported from outside the story's promise

Likely cause:

- no active payoff tracker
- setup lives in memory instead of in a stable document

## Genre Routing Rule

If the complaint sounds genre-specific, test that layer directly:

- "chemistry vanished" or "they keep circling" -> romance failures
- "the reveal was flat" or "the case feels fake" -> mystery failures
- "growth feels empty" or "power-ups feel weightless" -> progression fantasy failures
- "the politics are muddy" or "betrayals feel random" -> political intrigue failures
