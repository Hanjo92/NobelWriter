# Mystery Failures

Use this file when the manuscript's main promise depends on investigation, revelation, concealment, deduction, or suspense.

## Core Mystery Promise

The reader should feel that clues, suspicion, and revelation are being managed with control, fairness, and escalating consequence.

## Common Mystery Failure Modes

### Clue Without Leverage

Symptoms:

- clues appear, but they do not change theory, suspicion, or action
- discoveries add information volume without altering pressure

Likely cause:

- clues are being treated as content rather than as decision-changing evidence

### Fake Complexity

Symptoms:

- many names, factions, or red herrings appear, but the case does not feel deeper
- confusion rises faster than meaningful uncertainty

Likely cause:

- complication is replacing actual investigative progression

### Reveal Without Reframe

Symptoms:

- the reveal answers the question, but earlier scenes do not gain new meaning
- readers understand what happened but do not feel the revelation land

Likely cause:

- the reveal solves the plot mechanically without recontextualizing prior evidence

### Investigator Passivity

Symptoms:

- revelations happen to the protagonist rather than because of them
- the investigation advances through coincidence, villain error, or exposition dump

Likely cause:

- the inquiry engine is weak and does not force active hypothesis or risk

### Suspense Decoupled From Logic

Symptoms:

- scenes feel tense while reading, but later collapse under inspection
- characters withhold obvious questions or ignore available tests

Likely cause:

- tension is being generated by omission rather than by credible uncertainty

## QA Questions

- Does each clue alter suspicion, theory, or behavior
- Are red herrings fair and functional, not just noisy
- Does the reveal reframe earlier material
- Is the investigator making meaningful moves that create risk
- Can the tension survive basic logic checking

## Repair Rule

Fix mystery failures by restoring clue leverage, investigative agency, and reveal force. More obscurity is not the answer if the logic chain is weak.
