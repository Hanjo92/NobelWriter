# Political Intrigue Failures

Use this file when the manuscript's main promise depends on faction play, secrecy, leverage, betrayal, negotiation, or institutional power struggle.

## Core Political Promise

Power should move through decisions, alliances, information control, and visible consequence. The reader should feel that choices inside the system matter and produce reconfiguration.

## Common Political Intrigue Failure Modes

### Faction Blur

Symptoms:

- multiple factions exist, but their goals, methods, or risk tolerance feel interchangeable
- scenes mention political movement without changing the strategic map

Likely cause:

- faction identity is being described but not dramatized through competing incentives

### Betrayal Without Leverage

Symptoms:

- betrayals happen for shock, but they do not emerge from prior pressure or incentives
- reversals feel like plot twists instead of strategic outcomes

Likely cause:

- the story is prioritizing surprise over credible leverage build

### Scheme Without Consequence

Symptoms:

- plans and counterplans are discussed at length, but failure or success changes little
- maneuvering scenes create complexity without cost

Likely cause:

- strategy is being narrated rather than cashed through concrete loss, gain, or constraint

### Information Control Collapse

Symptoms:

- secrets spread only when the plot wants them to
- characters learn hidden facts without a believable chain of access
- blackmail, rumor, or surveillance loses consistency

Likely cause:

- knowledge state is not being tracked at the same level as faction movement

### Institutional Stage Weakness

Symptoms:

- the court, guild, council, clan, or ministry is said to matter but behaves like a backdrop
- formal roles do not constrain what characters can attempt

Likely cause:

- the institution has status flavor but weak procedural force

## QA Questions

- Are factions distinct at the level of incentive, not just name
- Does each maneuver alter the strategic map, trust map, or cost structure
- Are betrayals earned by pressure and leverage
- Is knowledge flow believable and tracked
- Do institutions constrain action or merely decorate it

## Repair Rule

Fix political-intrigue failures by restoring incentive clarity, leverage chains, and institutional consequence. More names, titles, or secret meetings will not help if power is not actually moving.
