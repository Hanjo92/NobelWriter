# POV And Tone Stability Checks

Use this file when the main risk is that point of view, narrative distance, or tonal control drifts across chapters, arcs, or emotional pressure changes.

## Core Principle

POV and tone instability is not only a prose problem. It is often a continuity problem in how the manuscript handles access, emphasis, and reader expectation.

## Common POV Failures

### Focal Drift

Symptoms:

- the narration begins in one character's access frame and quietly slides into another's knowledge, feeling, or judgment
- interior access expands or contracts without a clear scene-level reason

Likely cause:

- the manuscript is chasing convenience in exposition or emphasis
- scene goals were clear, but viewpoint discipline was not maintained

### Distance Instability

Symptoms:

- narration alternates unpredictably between close interiority and distant summary
- similar emotional beats receive radically different intimacy levels without design value

Likely cause:

- narrative distance is changing by sentence habit rather than by scene purpose

### Knowledge Leakage

Symptoms:

- the reader receives information that the active POV should not yet frame, infer, or notice
- suspense weakens because hidden facts leak through narration

Likely cause:

- author knowledge is overriding character-bound access

## Common Tone Failures

### Tonal Reversion

Symptoms:

- a chapter briefly establishes dread, urgency, romantic vulnerability, or political pressure, then narration returns to a neutral default too quickly
- aftermath scenes lose the emotional weather of the triggering event

Likely cause:

- scene-level tone is not being carried into follow-through chapters

### Genre Tone Blur

Symptoms:

- a mystery starts explaining too plainly
- a romance starts reporting emotion instead of staging it
- a progression fantasy starts sounding mechanically procedural without excitement
- a political intrigue story starts flattening conflict into generic confrontation

Likely cause:

- genre promise is no longer shaping emphasis, pacing, and narrative selection

### Register Mismatch

Symptoms:

- narration sounds more literary, comic, or casual than the surrounding chapter logic supports
- adjacent chapters feel as if written under different stylistic assumptions

Likely cause:

- tonal calibration is being reset chapter by chapter instead of carried as a project-level constraint

## QA Questions

- Who is the reader supposed to be closest to in this scene, and does the narration stay there
- What information should remain inaccessible at this point
- Is the narrative distance consistent with the scene's emotional job
- Does the aftermath chapter preserve the tonal weather created by the prior turning point
- Is the manuscript still sounding like the same genre promise it made early on

## Evidence Standard

When reporting POV or tone instability, name:

- the last chapter where the control still felt stable
- the first chapter where access or tone starts to slip
- the sentence-level or scene-level mechanism causing the slip
- the reader-visible damage

Do not call it unstable unless you can show both the prior baseline and the later breach.

## Repair Rule

Fix POV and tone instability by restoring control of access, distance, and tonal carryover. Prefer:

1. one clearly maintained focal frame per scene
2. consistent narrative distance for similar emotional functions
3. explicit protection against knowledge leakage
4. aftermath scenes that preserve tonal residue from major events

## Recheck Questions

- Does the scene now stay inside the intended access frame
- Has hidden information stopped leaking through narration
- Is the narrative distance stable across comparable scenes
- Does tone now carry through aftermath instead of resetting to neutral
- Does the manuscript still feel aligned with its declared genre promise
