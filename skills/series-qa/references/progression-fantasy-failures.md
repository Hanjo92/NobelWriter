# Progression Fantasy Failures

Use this file when the manuscript's main promise depends on visible growth, rank-up momentum, training payoff, power system friction, or long-form advancement.

## Core Progression Promise

Growth should feel earned, costly, legible, and exciting. Advancement must change what the protagonist can attempt, risk, or lose.

## Common Progression Failure Modes

### Growth Without Friction

Symptoms:

- new power arrives, but sacrifice, limitation, or adaptation is minimal
- training or breakthroughs feel pre-cleared rather than fought for

Likely cause:

- the cost structure is too soft or reversible

### Stat Increase Without Story Change

Symptoms:

- rank-ups happen, but scene structure and challenge texture stay the same
- the protagonist becomes stronger without facing qualitatively different problems

Likely cause:

- progression is being tracked numerically more than dramatically

### Training Loop Repetition

Symptoms:

- repeated train-fail-train-win beats stop producing excitement
- chapters differ in technique names but not in dramatic function

Likely cause:

- the advancement loop is repeating without new stakes, environment, or consequence

### System Inflation

Symptoms:

- new rules, titles, resources, or tiers keep being added, but clarity drops
- readers understand less as the system grows

Likely cause:

- expansion is outrunning readability and payoff control

### Power-Up Without Payment

Symptoms:

- a major breakthrough solves problems cleanly but costs little
- success narrows future tension instead of opening a harder field

Likely cause:

- advancement is rewarding the protagonist without creating a new strategic burden

## QA Questions

- Did the latest advancement change challenge type, not just challenge size
- Is the price of growth visible in body, status, resources, or relationships
- Are training arcs producing new pressure instead of repeating the same loop
- Can the reader still track the system without confusion fatigue
- Does each power-up widen the story's stakes instead of flattening them

## Repair Direction Rule

Report progression failures as needing cost, challenge transformation, and system clarity. Louder abilities do not repair a weak advancement engine.
