# Romance Failures

Use this file when the manuscript's main promise depends on romantic tension, emotional reciprocity, or relationship progression.

## Core Romance Promise

The relationship must not only exist. It must change through pressure, choice, risk, and emotional cost.

## Common Romance Failure Modes

### Chemistry Without Movement

Symptoms:

- scenes have attraction, but the relationship state does not change
- banter repeats without increasing vulnerability or consequence
- the pair keeps circling the same emotional beat

Likely cause:

- the story is preserving tension by freezing progress
- flirtation has replaced relational change

### Manufactured Delay

Symptoms:

- confessions or clarifications are delayed by avoidable misunderstanding
- near-confession scenes repeat with cosmetic variation
- obstacles feel inserted from outside the pair's real problem

Likely cause:

- the delay mechanism is not rooted in character fear, cost, or social reality

### Relationship Reset

Symptoms:

- a breakthrough scene happens, then the next arc behaves as if it did not
- jealousy, trust, or intimacy return to baseline too quickly

Likely cause:

- no lasting relational price or gain is being carried forward

### Uneven Emotional Risk

Symptoms:

- only one side seems to want, lose, or risk anything
- one character carries all the vulnerability while the other remains dramatically safe

Likely cause:

- the pair is not balanced at the level of emotional stakes

### Payoff Without Earned Build

Symptoms:

- confession, kiss, breakup, or reunion lands flat
- big relationship turns happen because the plot schedule demands it

Likely cause:

- the beat arrives without enough prior pressure, sacrifice, or internal shift

## QA Questions

- Did the pair's relationship state materially change in the sampled range
- Are delays rooted in character logic or in author convenience
- Does each major romantic beat impose new vulnerability, loss, or obligation
- Are both sides carrying visible emotional risk
- After a breakthrough, does later behavior reflect it

## Repair Direction Rule

Report romance failures as needing relational consequence, asymmetry, and irreversible emotional change. Do not frame more banter or more longing language as enough if the state itself is frozen.
